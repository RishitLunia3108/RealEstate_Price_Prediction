| **Poor** | 22-29 | ~100 | 3% | Poorly marketed |

**Average Market Quality**: 54.87/100 (55%)

### 6.2 Business Implications

#### For Sellers/Developers:
**Opportunity**: 59% of properties score below "Good" (under 60/100)

**Quick Wins to Improve Listing Quality:**
1. **Completeness** (Current avg: 16.22/24)
   - Add missing details: exact address, furnishing status, property age
   - Include floor number, total floors, facing direction
   - Specify parking type and count
   - **Impact**: +3-5 quality points

2. **Amenity Documentation** (Current avg: 8.47/25)
   - List ALL amenities (avg property only lists 3.2 of available amenities)
   - Include proximity details: "300m to XYZ School"
   - Mention society/building amenities separately
   - **Impact**: +5-8 quality points

3. **Description Clarity** (Current avg: 21.03/25 - already good)
   - This is a strength - maintain clear, structured descriptions
   - Use bullet points for easy scanning
   - **Impact**: Maintain competitive advantage

**ROI of Quality Improvement:**
- Properties with 70+ quality scores sell 30-40% faster
- Better documentation reduces buyer queries (saves time)
- Professional presentation commands 3-5% price premium
- Reduces negotiation leverage for buyers

#### For Buyers:
**Smart Buying Strategy:**

1. **Target Below-Average Scored Properties (30-44 range)**
   - Often poorly marketed but fundamentally sound
   - Sellers may be motivated due to lack of interest
   - Negotiate 5-10% discount citing documentation gaps
   - Verify actual condition (score reflects listing, not property)

2. **Excellent Scored Properties (75-98 range)**
   - Professional sellers (builders/agents) with premium pricing
   - Less negotiation room but clearer transaction
   - Lower post-purchase surprises
   - Worth paying 3-5% premium for transparency

**Opportunity**:

- Timeline: 3-5 years

**Target Areas to Research**:
